Introduction

An organizational policy on web accessibility defines your goals and targets for the @@ development and maintenance of accessible web content.

Accessibility policies can be standalone or integrated into other website policies or related documents, such as brand guidelines, coding standards, or project management frameworks. Including accessibility as part of general policies encourages an approach of accessibility as a core feature rather than an afterthought.

An organizational policy forms an important part of a broader strategic approach to accessibility. WAI provides guidance on how to succeed in that longer term goal in Strategic Planning for Web Accessibility.

Example of simple policy

ACME Inc. is committed to ensuring the accessibility of its website to people with disabilities. All the pages on our website will meet W3C WAI's Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.0, Level AA conformance. Any issues should be reported to accessibility@acme.inc.

What is this document for?

This document guides you through topics to consider when creating or updating an internal web accessibility policy. You may also consider publishing an external statement of intent for accessibility. It is not necessary to have statements for each topic. The topics covered are:

  • Reference Standards and Define Conformance Level
  • Define Scope of Policy
  • Set Conformance Milestones
  • Define Monitoring and Review Process

Reference Standards and Define Conformance Level

A standard on web accessibility defines what web content need to achieve in order to be considered accessible. W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) version 2.0 is an internationally recognized standard on web accessibility.

WCAG consists of guidelines, and related success criteria, for making content accessible. Success criteria are assigned to one of three levels of conformance; A, AA, and AAA. Level A is the minimum required level of conformance and Level AAA the highest level. Most organizations set their conformance goal as Level AA, since for some content it is not possible to satisfy all Level AAA Success Criteria.

Coordination with key website teams helps to set a goal that is achievable, depending on available resources and technical infrastructure.

Authoring tools used to create web content can impact the final accessibility of the content. Consider guidelines for your organization's content creation tools as an important part of your policy statement.

Note that you may wish to reference the latest version of W3C standards without specifying a version number. Doing so means you can always be certain of referring to the most up-to-date guidelines. Referencing a specific version will allow you to deliberately plan web content changes that may be required with new guidelines. Referencing is explained in Referencing Guidelines and Other Technical Specifications

What to include

  • Reference to the latest version of web content accessibility standards.
  • Conformance level to be achieved for each referenced standard.

Other considerations

  • Reference to the latest version of authoring tool standards.
  • Reference to any relevant legislative and regulatory requirements.

Relevant guidelines

  • Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) - Explains how to make web content more accessible to people with disabilities.
  • ISO/IEC 40500:2012 Information technology - ISO version of WCAG version 2.0.
  • Authoring Tool Accessibility Guidelines (ATAG) - how to make software better support the production of accessible web content.

Examples

  • ACME Inc. seeks to make sure our website conforms to W3C WAI WCAG Level AA.
  • At ACME Inc. we seek to ensure all of our websites and web applications, both customer-facing and for internal use, conform to all Level A success criteria of the W3C WAI WCAG Version 2.0. We also aim to ensure that our authoring tools conform to all Level A criteria of the ATAG Version 2.0.

Define Scope of Policy

Your organizational policy should clearly identify what website content it applies to. Consider to what extent the policy applies to new, updated, and existing web pages. Where your organization has multiple websites, the policy should make it clear which websites are addressed. You may need to consider how your policy applies to web content or components supplied by third-parties.

What to include

  • Specify what web content the policy applies to.
  • Specify how the policy relates to existing, updated, and new content.

Other considerations

  • Consider how the policy applies to third-party content and code providers.

Examples

  • This policy applies to all new, updated, and existing web pages.
  • This policy applies to all web content produced or updated by ACME Inc. In addition, ACME Inc. is taking the following steps to ensure accessibility of content provided by third-party developers:
    • Informing third-party developers of ACME Inc's policy on web accessibility;
    • Providing links to information and resources on implementing web accessibility;
    • Monitoring and providing feedback on inaccessible third-party content;
    • Preferential selection of providers of WCAG 2.0 Level AA conformant content;
    • Seeking alternative third-party content providers where original providers continue to provide non-conformant content.

Set Conformance Milestones

Where the web content in scope is known to fall short of the specified policy, define a date by which it will meet the policy. This helps ensure that focus is maintained on achieving policy goals.

When considering deadlines, be realistic, but also ensure that issues are not left too long. In some cases, it may be practical to phase in accessibility. A phased approach might first rapidly address significant accessibility barriers, then implement solutions for other issues as part of a planned maintenance update. Clearly outlined details of this type of approach in your policy.

For some websites, consider prioritizing based on important features or areas of the site — for example, the checkout process of an online store. It may also be appropriate to identify particular types of content, such as video, and specify when they will be accessible.

What to include

  • Specify when the policy goals will be met.

Other considerations

  • Consider whether to define a staged approach to fix absolute accessibility barriers more quickly.
  • Consider prioritizing important features and functions.

Examples

  • By February 27, 2015, ACME Inc's websites will meet WCAG 2.0, Level AA conformance.
  • By November 7, 2014, ACME Inc's websites will meet WCAG 2.0, Level A conformance; and by May 29, 2015 websites will meet WCAG 2.0, Level AA conformance.
  • By June 26, 2015, all vendors of authoring tools used by ACME Inc's should provide information regarding their plans for ATAG 2.0 conformance in future versions of their software. By June 24, 2016 ACME Inc. will preferentially purchase ATAG conformant authoring tools.

Define Monitoring and Review Process

Progress towards policy goals should be reviewed regularly. Any changes in timescale or milestones that have been met should be updated in the policy.

Specify a recommended process and schedule for reviewing web content in scope to ensure that the target level of accessibility is maintained.

Feedback mechanisms are important to support users who might have accessibility difficulties with your site and to help gather information on barriers. Your policy should include information on what mechanisms will be included and how feedback is to be handled and responded to.

What to include

  • Specify a regular review period and process.
  • Outline how user feedback will be captured, responded to, and used.

Examples

  • All areas of the ACME Inc. website will be reviewed annually using the process described at Website Accessibility Conformance Evaluation Methodology. Reviews are the responsibility of identified departmental owners. Accessibility checks will be incorporated into the publishing workflow for all new content.
  • Each page of the website will include a link to a form allowing users to submit feedback on the site; this information will be compiled and considered during the review process. Any submitted feedback will be responded to within 48 hours.

Example Policies

Example of simple policy

ACME Inc. is committed to ensuring the accessibility of its website to people with disabilities. All of the content on our website will meet W3C WAI's Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.0, Level AA conformance. Any issues should be reported to accessibility@acme.inc.

Example of in-depth policy

ACME Inc. is committed to ensuring the accessibility of its website to people with disabilities. New and updated web content produced by our organization will meet W3C WAI's Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.0, Level A conformance, by February 27, 2015.

Existing web content produced by our organization, and new, updated, and existing web content provided for our site by third-party developers, will meet Level AA conformance by November 27, 2015.

We will initiate an internal monitoring program by June 26, 2015. Vendors supplying software used to develop our site will be required to provide information by June 24, 2016, on conformance to W3C WAI's Authoring Tool Accessibility Guidelines 2.0, Conformance Level A.

Our home page and our 'about this site' page will include a link to a public version of this policy.

All of our web pages will include a link to our 'Feedback' form. The form will include an option to indicate the feedback is related to an accessibility barrier or issue. Any submitted feedback will be responded to by our customer services team within 48 hours. Feedback related to accessibility issues will be passed to our Web Management Team for assessment.

We will review this policy in the future to consider updating it to reference later versions of W3C's accessibility guidelines.

Next steps: Maintaining your policy

Your policy may include milestones and references to particular standards by version number. Review the information contained within your policy regularly to ensure that it is accurate and up-to-date.

What to consider

  • Identify who is responsible for maintaining the policy.
  • Specify a regular review period.

Long term: Strategic Planning

An accessibility policy is one part of a broader strategic approach to accessibility. Making accessibility an integral part of your web development strategy is more cost effective and efficient than considering it in isolation. There are several things you can do to help your organization create more accessible websites as standard.

Strategic Planning for Web Accessibility outlines how to integrate accessibility throughout your web development. Ensure that when redesigns or updates are planned, accessibility is included from the start of the project. Accessibility is much less costly and time-consuming when tackled at the beginning of a project, rather than the end.
